BIRDS

The first classification of birds was developed by Francis Willughby and John Ray in their 1676 volume Ornithologiae. Carl Linnaeus modified that work in 1758 to devise the taxonomic classification system currently in use. Birds are categorised as the biological class Aves in Linnaean taxonomy.

ELEPHANT

The word "elephant" is based on the Latin elephas, which is the Latinised form of the Greek ἐλέφας, probably from a non-Indo-European language, likely Phoenician. It is attested in Mycenaean Greek as e-re-pa in Linear B syllabic script.As Mycenaean Greek, Homer used the Greek word to mean ivory.
